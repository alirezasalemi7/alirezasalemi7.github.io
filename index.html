Alireza Salemi

PhD Candidate · University of Massachusetts Amherst

Center for Intelligent Information Retrieval · Advisor: Hamed Zamani

🎓 Google PhD Fellow  ·  🏆 SIGIR Best Paper ×2

I build systems at the intersection of information retrieval and large language models, focusing on three areas: making LLMs retrieve and use external knowledge more reliably (RAG), adapting LLMs to individual users' history and preferences (personalization), and composing LLMs into agentic systems for multi-step reasoning and autonomous information seeking. A common thread is grounding model behavior in evidence rather than parametric memory. My work has appeared at ACL ('24–'26), SIGIR ('23–'26), EMNLP ('21, '25), ICTIR ('23, '25), ICLR ('26), and WWW ('26).
